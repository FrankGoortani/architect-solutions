How Generative-AI Start-ups Build — and Keep — Defensive Moats

Frank Goortani

Understanding AI Moats

Moats include data flywheels, distribution lock-in, regulatory advantages and compute barriers. The strongest companies combine several moat types.

Real-World Examples

OpenAI pairs massive user data with Microsoft distribution. Anthropic focuses on safety and compute partnerships. Cohere leverages privacy and compliance, while Perplexity gains distribution through browser integrations.

VC Insights on Defensibility

Investors emphasize product loops, customer integration and safety leadership as key signals of defensibility.

Five-Step Playbook

  1. Choose a core moat early.
  2. Turn user activity into a data advantage.
  3. Secure privileged compute access.
  4. Leverage regulation strategically.
  5. Expand distribution quickly.

Avoiding Pitfalls

Don't open-source too early, rely on a single channel or neglect MLOps and governance. Dual licenses, diversified distribution and robust operations protect moats.

Strategic Checklist for Founders

  • Audit existing moats and address weaknesses.
  • Secure compute agreements early.
  • Prioritize trade secrets over patents.
  • Track moat metrics to maintain advantages.

Conclusion

Layered strategies across data, distribution, regulation and compute create durable moats. Executed well, they convince investors and customers alike.
