AI, Agile, and the Return of Detailed Planning: A New Balance for Leaders

Frank Goortani

Introduction: When AI Meets Agile Development

Teams that embraced fast moving Agile methods now write exhaustive specifications before turning work over to AI coding assistants. Detailed plans help models follow intent, turning English into the hottest new programming language.

Why AI Is Changing the Rules

Generative AI excels at deterministic execution but fails when requirements are vague. Clear, unambiguous specs unlock efficiency gains of up to three times, while fuzzy prompts yield mediocre code.

The Lure of Detailed Specs

Some teams now spend triple the time on design, producing PRDs that read like legal contracts. Tools such as Amazon's Kiro enforce spec-first workflows, delivering code that works on the first try.

Why Clarity Was Always King

Agile never banned planning; it rejected rigid plans. AI exposes vague thinking and pushes teams to externalize knowledge for machine consumption. Good documentation is an ally, not an enemy.

The Risks of Overcorrecting

Overly rigid specs can still produce the wrong product. Maintain short feedback loops and validate each increment with users. Plan in detail for small chunks, then adjust as new insights emerge.

“Waterfall 2.0” – A Hybrid Approach

The future blends structured documentation with iterative delivery. Specs, design docs and tests become first-class artifacts consumed by AI agents throughout the lifecycle.

Leadership Perspective

Leaders must promote clear specs while preserving agility. Documentation should be a living document, revised as feedback arrives. Human oversight and quality assurance remain critical.

Conclusion

AI doesn't kill Agile; it kills vague thinking. Balance meticulous planning with continuous feedback to harness AI's speed without losing customer focus.
